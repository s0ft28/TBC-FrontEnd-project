Waterfowl Hunting

Learn the traditions and techniques of hunting ducks, geese, and other waterfowl.

What is Waterfowl Hunting?

Waterfowl hunting is the practice of hunting ducks, geese, and other water birds. It's one of the most traditional forms of hunting in North America, with a rich heritage dating back centuries.

This type of hunting typically involves setting up decoys on water or in fields, using calls to attract birds, and shooting from blinds or layout boats. It requires understanding bird behavior, weather patterns, and migration routes.

Common Waterfowl Species

Mallard

The most recognized and popular duck species

Canada Goose

Large, abundant, and challenging to hunt

Wood Duck

Colorful and prized for their beauty

Teal

Fast-flying, early season favorites

Pintail

Elegant ducks with distinctive markings

Snow Goose

Often hunted in large numbers during migration

Essential Gear

  • Shotgun: 12-gauge is most popular, with 20-gauge growing in popularity. Must be plugged to hold only 3 shells.
  • Decoys: A spread of 6-12 decoys is a good starting point. Mix drakes and hens.
  • Calls: Duck calls and goose calls to attract passing birds. Practice is essential!
  • Waders: Chest or hip waders for setting decoys and retrieving birds.
  • Blind: Layout blind, boat blind, or permanent blind for concealment.

Hunting Techniques

Decoy Spreads

Set up realistic patterns that mimic feeding or resting birds. Leave a landing zone.

Calling

Use greeting calls, feed calls, and comeback calls. Less is often more.

Pass Shooting

Position along flight paths and take shots as birds pass overhead.

Jump Shooting

Stalk ponds and waterways, flushing birds for quick shots.

Quick Facts

  • Season Fall and winter, following migration
  • Best Locations Marshes, lakes, rivers, flooded fields
  • License Required State license + Federal Duck Stamp

Beginner Tips

  • • Steel shot is required - lead is banned for waterfowl
  • • Arrive before legal shooting time to set up
  • • Concealment is critical - stay hidden!
  • • Learn to identify species before shooting
  • • A retriever dog is invaluable but not required
