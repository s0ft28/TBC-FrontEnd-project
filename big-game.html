Big Game Hunting

Master the art of pursuing deer, elk, moose, and other large game animals.

What is Big Game Hunting?

Big game hunting refers to the pursuit of large wild animals, typically for sport, food, or wildlife management. In North America, this includes species like white-tailed deer, mule deer, elk, moose, bear, and wild boar.

This form of hunting requires patience, skill, and respect for the animal. It's often considered the most challenging and rewarding type of hunting due to the size, intelligence, and wariness of the quarry.

Popular Big Game Species

White-tailed Deer

Most popular game animal in North America

Elk

Majestic animals found in western regions

Moose

Largest member of the deer family

Black Bear

Found across many US states and Canada

Mule Deer

Common in western United States

Wild Boar

Invasive species open year-round in many areas

Essential Gear

  • Rifle or Bow: Choose based on your skill level and local regulations. Popular calibers include .308, .30-06, and .270 for rifles.
  • Optics: Quality binoculars (8x42 or 10x42) and a reliable rifle scope are essential.
  • Clothing: Layered, scent-controlled clothing appropriate for the weather.
  • Field Dressing Kit: Sharp knives, game bags, and rope for processing your harvest.

Hunting Techniques

Stand Hunting

Position yourself in a tree stand or ground blind along known travel routes.

Spot and Stalk

Glass open areas, locate game, and carefully approach within shooting range.

Still Hunting

Move slowly and quietly through the habitat, stopping frequently to observe.

Calling

Use grunt calls, rattling antlers, or cow calls to attract animals during rut.

Quick Facts

  • Season Fall through early winter (varies by state)
  • Best Locations Forests, mountains, agricultural edges
  • License Required State hunting license + species tags

Beginner Tips

  • • Start with deer - they're the most accessible big game
  • • Scout your hunting area before the season
  • • Practice shooting from realistic positions
  • • Learn to read animal sign (tracks, rubs, scrapes)
  • • Take a hunter education course
