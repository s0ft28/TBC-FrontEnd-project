beginner tips

Starting your hunting journey? Here's everything you need to know to get started safely and successfully.

Getting Started Essentials

Hunter Safety Course

Required in most states. Learn firearm safety, ethics, and regulations.

  • Complete your state's hunter education course
  • Many courses are available online
  • Keep your certification card with you

Know the Regulations

Every state has different seasons, bag limits, and legal requirements.

  • Get a copy of your state's hunting regulations
  • Understand season dates for your target species
  • Know legal shooting hours and methods

Scout Your Area

Success starts before hunting season with proper scouting.

  • Use maps to identify public hunting lands
  • Look for game signs: tracks, droppings, rubs
  • Set up trail cameras if allowed

Find a Mentor

Learning from experienced hunters accelerates your progress.

  • Join local hunting clubs or organizations
  • Ask at sporting goods stores for programs
  • Many states have mentored hunting programs

Getting Started Essentials

1 Always treat every firearm as if it's loaded
2 Keep the muzzle pointed in a safe direction
3 Keep your finger off the trigger until ready to shoot
4 Be sure of your target and what's beyond it
5 Wear blaze orange during required seasons
6 Tell someone your hunting plan and expected return
7 Carry a first aid kit and emergency supplies
8 Check weather conditions before heading out

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not practicing with your firearm

Spend time at the range before season

Wearing the wrong clothing

Layer properly and dress for conditions

Being too loud in the field

Move slowly and minimize noise

Hunting without proper licenses

Double-check all required permits

Ignoring wind direction

Always hunt with wind in your face

Giving up too early

Patience is the #1 hunting skill
