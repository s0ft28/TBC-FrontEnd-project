Choosing Your First Hunting Firearm

Selecting the right firearm is one of the most important decisions a hunter makes. Your choice depends on what you're hunting, where you're hunting, and your personal preferences.

RIFLES

Precision at distance

Rifles fire a single projectile with high accuracy over long distances. Ideal for big game hunting where precision shots are necessary.

  • Deer, elk, moose hunting
  • Long-range accuracy (100-500+ yards)
  • Single, precise shots
  • Bolt-action most popular for hunting

SHOTGUNS

Versatile spread

Shotguns fire multiple pellets in a spread pattern, making them perfect for fast-moving targets and close-range hunting.

  • Waterfowl and upland birds
  • Close to medium range (under 50 yards)
  • Moving targets
  • Pump, semi-auto, and break-action types

Firearm Safety Rules

Treat every firearm as if it's loaded
Never point at anything you don't intend to shoot
Keep your finger off the trigger until ready to shoot
Be sure of your target and what's beyond it
Store firearms securely when not in use
Always wear eye and ear protection
